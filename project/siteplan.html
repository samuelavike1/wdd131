Site Name

Campus Study Planner — a student-focused toolkit that consolidates study tips, a weekly schedule template, a to-do list, and a simple GPA/grade calculator in one responsive web app.

Why this name? It’s descriptive, memorable, and clearly signals the site’s value to college and high-school students.

Optional domain availability: campusstudyplanner.me or studyplanner-campus.site (domain purchase not required for class).

Site Purpose

The site serves as a compact hub for students to plan their week, track tasks, and estimate academic standing. It provides:

  • Study Tips: short, actionable cards on time management, active recall, spaced repetition, and focus.
  • Weekly Schedule: a Mon–Sun grid with add/edit task forms; supports print and clear week actions.
  • To-Do List: add/edit/delete tasks with priority and due date; filters for All/Today/Completed; stored in localStorage.
  • GPA/Grade Calculator: enter courses, credits, and grades to compute current and projected GPA with validation.

This scope enables practice with semantic HTML/CSS, responsive layout, event-driven JavaScript, and simple data persistence.

Scenarios

  • “How can I visualize my week quickly?” A weekly grid lets me place class and study blocks with start/end times and color coding.
  • “What GPA will I have if I score a B+ in Math?” The calculator shows updated GPA and target scores for goals.
  • “What should I focus on today?” The To-Do list filter surfaces Today’s items and highlights overdue tasks.

Color Schema

Two core colors with clear roles; shown as live chips and used in headings/accents:

Primary — #1E3A8A
Headings, links, key outlines, emphasis
Accent — #F59E0B
Buttons, highlights, badges, focus states

Neutral palette uses white background with slate grays for body text and borders for strong contrast and readability.

Typography

Headings (Display)

Playfair Display, 700

Used for H1–H2 to give distinct visual hierarchy and brand character.

Body & UI

Inter, 400/600 — used for paragraphs, lists, labels, buttons, and form inputs for clarity and legibility.

Testing & Self-Check

  • HTML Validator: Passes without errors/warnings (W3C).
  • Accessibility: Landmarks, labels, keyboard focus, sufficient contrast (check with Lighthouse/axe).
  • Best Practices: No console errors; descriptive titles/alt text; semantic headings.
  • Performance: Simple CSS, system/Google fonts only; minimal layout shift.
  • SEO basics: Descriptive <title>, headings, and meaningful copy.
  • Responsive: Layout adapts from ~320px up to desktop; readable tap targets.
